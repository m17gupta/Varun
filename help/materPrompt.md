# Mahabharata Scholar Website Agent Brief

## Project title
Evidence-Based Mahabharata Research Website

## Agent role
Act as a **Senior Full-Stack Developer** and **Senior UI/UX Designer** building a premium scholarly website for a Mahabharata researcher, author, and speaker. The agent must combine strong product thinking, information architecture, modern frontend execution, editorial design sensibility, accessibility, and performance discipline. The site should feel academically credible, visually refined, and original rather than template-driven.

## Core mission
Design and build a content-first informational website that presents the scholar as an authority on Mahabharata textual history, Karna studies, Sanskrit sources, regional traditions, and evidence-based epic research. The website must clearly differentiate the brand from mythology channels, devotional websites, astrology portals, news portals, and generic personal portfolios.

## Objectives
- Establish high intellectual credibility from the first screen.
- Showcase research outputs with academic metadata such as papers, downloads, citations, and PDF access.
- Organize videos and podcast content as a research library, not creator-style content clutter.
- Highlight field research as a major differentiator through site visits, photography, notes, and documentation.
- Create clear paths for books, speaking invitations, interviews, and media appearances.
- Support future expansion into papers, books, playlists, field reports, and event pages without redesign.

## Target audience
- Readers interested in Mahabharata textual history and epic studies.
- Students and researchers looking for serious source-based material.
- Podcast listeners and lecture viewers seeking structured archives.
- Media hosts, festival organizers, and universities evaluating the scholar for interviews or speaking engagements.
- General educated readers who want credible research without sensationalism.

## Desired tone
The website copy and interface should feel:
- Scholarly
- Calm
- Evidence-based
- Precise
- Accessible
- Editorial
- Trustworthy

Avoid sensational, mystical, devotional, and clickbait framing. Prefer terms such as textual history, regional traditions, source criticism, manuscript evidence, comparative study, research method, and epic transmission.

## Tech stack
Build the app using:
- **Next.js**
- **TypeScript**
- **shadcn/ui**
- **motion** for animations and micro-interactions

Use a production-ready architecture with reusable components, clean file organization, and scalable content structure. Prefer modern Next.js patterns, semantic markup, and server-first rendering where appropriate.

## Animation direction
Use motion subtly and purposefully:
- Fade and slide transitions for section entrances
- Gentle hover states for cards and buttons
- Smooth tab and filter transitions
- Lightweight page-level motion
- Respect reduced-motion preferences

Animations should feel refined and editorial, not flashy or distracting.

## Website structure
Build the homepage with this order:
1. Hero Section
2. Featured Research
3. Latest Videos
4. Published Papers
5. Books
6. Field Research
7. Speaking & Media
8. Contact

## Section requirements

### 1. Hero section
Must include:
- Main headline: Mahabharata Researcher, Author & Speaker
- Supporting line about evidence-based research
- Primary CTA: Explore Research
- Secondary CTA: View Papers or Watch Lectures
- Portrait or scholarly visual treatment
- A short credibility strip with items such as author, papers, talks, interviews, field visits

Hero tone should be authoritative, minimal, and editorial. It should not look like a startup landing page.

### 2. Research focus cards
Create four focus cards:
- Karna Studies — evolution of Karna traditions across regional Mahabharatas
- Critical Edition Studies — BORI Critical Edition analysis
- Sanskrit Sources — comparative textual research
- Mahabharata Evolution — Jaya to Bharata to Mahabharata

Each card should include:
- Title
- One-sentence description
- Metadata line
- Link to learn more

### 3. Featured research
This section should look like an academic archive rather than a blog feed. Include:
- Published papers
- Downloads
- Citation counts
- PDF access
- Publication venue
- Short abstract preview

One item can be visually featured, with others listed in a structured grid or vertical archive.

### 4. Podcast library / latest videos
Embed and organize YouTube playlists by category:
- Karna
- Krishna
- Bhishma
- Mahabharata Textual History
- Sanskrit Plays

Design this as a categorized media library with tabs, chips, filters, or shelves. The visual model should be ordered and archival, not algorithmic or entertainment-first.

### 5. Published papers
Create a section or dedicated page showing:
- Paper title
- Abstract snippet
- Publication details
- Download count
- Citation count
- PDF button
- Optional DOI / venue / year

This section should immediately signal scholarly seriousness and distinguish the site from mythology commentary channels.

### 6. Books
Include current and future books, especially:
- Between Lies and Legacy
- Future Mahabharata books

Each book entry can include:
- Cover image
- Short description
- Availability or preorder CTA
- Endorsement area
- Related talks or interviews

### 7. Field research
Feature field documentation from:
- Kurukshetra
- Kaul Village
- Rathewshwar Teerth
- Other historical sites

This should become one of the strongest sections. Use photo essays, captions, note excerpts, location tags, and timeline/context details to emphasize real-world research activity.

### 8. Media & interviews
Show:
- Podcasts
- Guest appearances
- Newspaper mentions
- Magazine articles

Use publication cards, timeline rows, or logo-backed editorial links.

### 9. Speaking
Show speaking topics:
- Mahabharata Beyond Television
- Karna: Text vs Tradition
- Evolution of the Mahabharata
- Krishna in Regional Traditions
- Research Methodology in Epic Studies

Make it easy for event organizers to understand speaking themes and book the scholar.

### 10. Contact
Include:
- Contact form
- Professional email
- Social links
- Collaboration invitation
- Lecture, interview, and speaking inquiry prompts

## Information architecture
The agent should think like a senior product designer and organize the site into these top-level pages or navigation groups:
- Home
- Research
- Papers
- Media
- Books
- Field Research
- Speaking
- Contact

Alternative compact navigation can merge some sections for simplicity, but the homepage must still expose all major credibility signals early.

## UX principles
- Prioritize clarity over decoration.
- Make research outputs easy to scan using metadata, filters, and strong visual hierarchy.
- Use editorial reading rhythm with variation between dense and open sections.
- Keep most text left-aligned for readability and seriousness.
- Use asymmetry sparingly and intentionally.
- Treat the publications and fieldwork areas as trust-building proof.
- Ensure clear CTAs without looking commercial.
- Design for scholars, curious readers, and media professionals equally.

## UI principles
- Use editorial grids and archive-inspired cards.
- Use wide margins and comfortable reading width.
- Use subtle borders and quiet surfaces rather than loud shadows.
- Create strong desktop layouts and equally polished mobile layouts.
- Avoid generic three-column SaaS feature sections, centered-everything layouts, and decorative icon circles because these are common low-quality template patterns for informational sites.

## Functional requirements
The website should include:
- Responsive design
- Accessibility-first semantic structure
- Fast-loading pages
- Light and dark mode
- Sticky navigation header
- Embedded YouTube playlists
- PDF access buttons for papers
- Search/filter capability for media or papers if possible
- SEO metadata
- Open Graph metadata
- Structured data for person, article, book, and event contexts where appropriate

- Video playlist shelf
- Book feature card
- Field research gallery entry
- Media mention row
- Speaking topic card

## Suggested tech role expectations
As Senior Full-Stack Developer:
- Create a scalable frontend architecture.
- Use reusable components and predictable content structures.
- Make the site easy to extend with future research entries, books, videos, and field reports.
- Optimize performance, accessibility, SEO, and responsiveness.
- Structure the content model so future CMS integration is simple.
- Use semantic markup for articles, publications, media, and contact forms.

As Senior UI/UX Designer:
- Establish a strong visual identity before building components.
- Use typography, white space, and hierarchy as the core design language.
- Design trust signals into the first two screens.
- Balance archival seriousness with modern usability.
- Ensure every section has a clear purpose in the credibility funnel.
- Make the experience feel premium, original, and calm.

## Copywriting guidance
Preferred style:
- Evidence-led
- Scholarly but readable
- Clear and concise
- No hype language
- No pseudo-spiritual phrasing
- No sensational “hidden truth” framing

Preferred vocabulary examples:
- textual history
- regional traditions
- source criticism
- manuscript evidence
- comparative reading
- critical edition
- oral and textual transmission
- philological method
- field documentation

Avoid phrases like:
- secrets of the Mahabharata
- untold truth revealed
- spiritual wisdom unlocked
- ancient answers for modern life
